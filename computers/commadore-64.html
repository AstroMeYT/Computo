Commadore 64

The Commadore 64 was a very popular, actually the best selling, computer of all time. It reigned in the 1980s and the early 90s
crushing sales like never before. It's populatiry came from its ability of berign versatile. Its main language
was BASIC, but people created other languages for it. It had support for cassette tapes, floppy disks, cartridges, and other media formats.
Because of its popularity, there were many programs for it too. From office to games, you could find anything. The system was particularly small,
having only 64 KB of RAM, and no built-in storage. Developers had to be effecient with their code.

Details and Specs:
Release year: 1982
Peak in Sales: 1983-1985
Price when released: $595
RAM: 64 KB
Storage: NONE
Clock speed: 1.023 Mhz (NTSC)
